WHAT IS THE LEP?

The Legislative Effectiveness Project (LEP) is a joint research project that seeks to understand which members of the United States Congress are the most effective at lawmaking. We use a precise research methodology to calculate a Legislative Effectiveness Score for each member of the House of Representatives, where the average score in each two-year Congress is equal to 1. In the webpages that follow, you can learn how effective your Representative was during the 112th Congress (2011-2012), or see any other Representative between the 93rd and 112th Congresses (1973-2012).

THE BOOK & AUTHORS

The Legislative Effectiveness Project is directed by Craig Volden of the University of Virginia, and Alan E. Wiseman of Vanderbilt University. In engaging the LEP, Volden and Wiseman have undertaken numerous research projects, including the recently published Legislative Effectiveness in the United States Congress: The Lawmakers (Cambridge University Press, 2014).

FIND REPRESENTATIVES

We have developed a simple and fun way for you to navigate our data set, including an interactive map, to quickly search Representatives' Legislative Effectiveness Scores. Additionally, we provide all of our data for download, for those who might be interested in further investigations of legislative effectiveness in the United States Congress.
