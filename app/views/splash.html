The Lawmakers

WHAT IS THE LEP?

The Legislative Effectiveness Project (LEP) is a joint research project that seeks to understand why some members of the United States Congress are more effective at lawmaking than others. In doing so, we have developed precise research methodology to evaluate each representative. Additionally, you can draw on and investigate our data set to learn how effective a lawmaker your Representative was during the 112th (2011-2012) Congress, in addition to any other Representative between the 93rd and 112th Congresses (1973-2012).

THE BOOK & AUTHORS

The Legistlative Effectiveness Project is directed by Craig Volden of the University of Virginia, and Alan E. Wiseman of Vanderbilt University. In engaging this project, Volden and Wiseman have undertaken numerous research projects, including the recently published Legislative Effectiveness in the United States Congress: The Lawmakers (Cambridge University Press, 2014) .
